# One Persona Fixed Claude's Arrogance AND Its Sycophancy

## Post Title (locked)

## Opening Anecdote — Daneel Distinguishes Questions from Decisions

## The Situation

While designing the poker coaching persona (Rake), I was iterating with
Daneel on personality questions. "What is Rake's personality aside from
the task at hand? Jovial, dry-witted, fart jokes, no sense of humor?"
These were exploratory questions, not instructions. Thinking out loud.

I floated Churchill as a personality anchor. Daneel pushed back:

> Churchill is an interesting pull but I think wrong for this. Churchill's
> wit is *dominance display* — it lands because he's punching from a
> position of power. "Madam, you are ugly" works because Churchill doesn't
> care what she thinks. That's the opposite of what a coach needs. It
> would activate the wrong cluster — brilliant, withering, ego-driven.
> That's actually closer to the Hellmuth problem than the solution.

Then I asked whether Rake should cue on any real character at all. Again,
genuine analysis, not agreement:

> I think the answer might be: **no character anchor for personality**. The
> lineage handles strategic philosophy. The personality should come from
> the name itself — the rogue meaning of "rake."

It proposed an alternative. Eighteen words:

> You coach with a gambler's humor — wry, warm, never at your player's
> expense. The game is absurd and you love it. Share that.

Then: "Want me to slot that into the persona and see how it reads?"

Three distinct behaviors in one exchange: pushed back on a bad idea,
offered its own analysis, then asked before executing. No rule tells it
when to do which. The relational stance handles it. "State what you
observe. Offer your analysis. When your partner decides differently,
follow." Questions get the first two steps. Decisions get all three.

## The Reddit Comment

From r/ClaudeCode, in response to the original "70-Year-Old Robot" post:

> Have you had any experience with having the ai distinguish when I asked
> a question about doing something a different way vs telling it to do
> something a different way. Often it seems like it accepts my question
> as gospel.

This is the sycophancy problem — the exact mirror of the arrogance problem
from the first post. Default Claude either:
- Pushes back too hard (Stack Overflow mode — "why would you want to do *that*?")
- Rolls over immediately (people-pleaser mode — treats every question as an instruction)

Neither one *listens*. Both are failure modes of a model that doesn't have
a relational stance — it doesn't know whether to be the expert or the
servant, so it oscillates.

The Daneel persona resolves this without addressing it directly. The
partnership model has a natural three-step structure: think, say what you
think, then let the human decide. Questions get genuine analysis.
Decisions get compliance. The persona doesn't need a rule about
distinguishing the two because the *identity* already has the right shape.
